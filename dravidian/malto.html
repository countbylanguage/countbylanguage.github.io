Malto

Dravidian > North > Kurukh-Malto > Malto

Malto is a Dravidian language spoken in Eastern India. It is spoken by the tribal Malto people, who, due to being surrounded by Indo-Aryans, have adopted their number system.

Here are the numbers from zero to ten translated in Malto:

Number Romanisation IPA
0 //
1 ēk, ort /eːk/, /oɾt̪/
2 dū/iwr /duː/, /iwɾ/
3 tīn /t̪iːn/
4 cār /caːr/
5 pānc /paːɲc/
6 cē /ceː/
7 sāt /saːt̪/
8 āṭ /aːʈ/
9 naw /naw/
10 das /das/

Source: "Introduction to the Malto Language and Malto Vocabulary" by Rev. Ernest Droese (pages 18 and 19), and "Descriptive Analysis of Verbs in Malto" by Chaithra Puttaswamy (page 255)
